# Full Site Audit — Loudoun Decks (ldndecks.com)

I analyzed 25+ pages across the site, including the homepage, about, all service pages, location pages, blog, showcase, contact, FAQs, team, robots.txt, sitemap, and the 404 page. Here is the complete analysis by category:

---

## 1. TECHNICAL SEO — Critical Site-Wide Issues

**Duplicate `<title>` tag brand name:** Nearly all pages repeat the brand name — for example "Deck Resurfacing Services | Loudoun Decks | Loudoun Decks". This pattern repeats across 10+ pages. It wastes valuable title characters and looks unprofessional in SERPs.

**Canonical tags are MISSING on ALL pages** except the homepage (which correctly has a canonical pointing to ldndecks.com). This is a serious issue — especially since the site runs on a Vercel subdomain (ldn-decks-next.vercel.app) that could be indexed as a duplicate.

**Generic meta description on blog posts:** All blog articles share the same generic site-wide meta description ("Premier custom deck builder in Northern Virginia..."). Each article must have a unique meta description.

**Homepage meta description too long:** 211 characters — Google truncates at ~155-160. It needs to be shortened.

**Limited Schema Markup:** The entire site only has LocalBusiness schema. Missing: ArticleSchema/BlogPosting on blog posts, FAQPage schema on FAQ sections (homepage, services, contact — all have FAQ accordions), Service schema on service pages, BreadcrumbList schema (even though breadcrumbs are visually present).

**og:type = "website" everywhere** — on blog articles it should be "article".

**og:url = homepage on blog posts** — each page needs its own og:url.

**Sitemap:** Exists and correctly references ldndecks.com. Robots.txt is OK (Allow: /, Disallow: /private/). Well configured.

---

## 2. CONTENT — Massive Inconsistency Across Pages

There is a huge imbalance in content depth:

**Strong pages (1000+ words):** /services/new-decks (~1400+ words, detailed FAQs, information about permits, materials, process — excellent), /services/porches (~1445 words), /services/fence (~1473 words).

**Weak pages (under 400 words):** /services/deck-resurfacing (374 words), /services/patios (289 words), /services/gazebo-pergola (302 words), /services/entry-doors (288 words), /services/deck-washing (329 words), /about (270 words), /about/why-choose-us (~350 words).

**Blog:** Only 4 articles, all under 170 words. This is very problematic — articles under 300 words rarely rank on Google. The /services/new-decks page has 10x more content than any blog article.

**Recommendation:** All service pages should be brought up to the level of the New Decks page (1000-1500+ words), with sections about materials, costs, process, Northern Virginia specifics, and dedicated FAQs. Blog articles need to be 800-1500 words.

---

## 3. LOCATION PAGES — Duplicate Content (Template-Based)

I checked Ashburn, Reston, Vienna, and Woodbridge. They all have the exact same structure, the same H2 headings, the same sections, ~605 words each. The only difference is the city name mechanically swapped:

Ashburn: "Loudoun Decks is the premier custom deck builder in Ashburn, VA."
Vienna: "Loudoun Decks is the premier custom deck builder in Vienna, VA."
Woodbridge: "...in Woodbridge, VA."

You have ~40+ location pages built this way. Google may penalize this type of thin/duplicate content as "doorway pages."

**Recommendation:** Each location page should contain unique content: projects completed in that city (linked to the showcase), local specifics (city-specific HOAs, local building regulations), testimonials from clients in that area, references to specific neighborhoods/communities. County pages (Loudoun County, Fairfax County, Prince William County) at only 433 words are thin — these should be the most robust local pages.

## 5. HOMEPAGE — Solid, But With Opportunities

Positives: strong hero image with clear CTA, service sections, testimonials (3 reviews), FAQs, integrated contact form, NAP information (name, address, phone), persistent "Call Now" button, skip link for accessibility, LocalBusiness schema.

Issues found: H1 = "Outdoor Deck & Fence Builder" — doesn't include location. It would be better as "Custom Deck & Fence Builder in Northern Virginia" or similar. The H2 heading "Best Services We'reProviding You" has a formatting error (the line is broken with a newline, creating "We'reProviding" — missing space). Form inputs lack `name` attributes (only have `type`) — this can cause issues with form processing. Testimonials lack Review/AggregateRating schema. The statistics on the showcase page ("0+ DECKS INSTALLED", "0+ SERVICES") literally show "0+" — the counter animation isn't working or the content isn't populated.

---


## 7. BLOG — Underdeveloped

4 articles, all under 170 words, no H2 headings, no supplementary images in the body, no contextual internal links, no ArticleSchema/BlogPosting schema, generic meta descriptions on all of them. The blog in its current state provides no SEO value and doesn't serve as an informational resource.

**Recommendation:** Each existing article needs to be expanded to 800-1500 words with structured H2/H3 headings. Suggested new article targets: "How Much Does a Deck Cost in Northern Virginia 2026?", "Trex vs TimberTech vs AZEK: Complete Comparison", "Do I Need a Permit for a Deck in Loudoun County?", "Best Deck Designs for Sloped Backyards", "How to Maintain Your Composite Deck" — all with high SEO potential and commercial/informational intent.

## 9. NAVIGATION & STRUCTURE

Positives: clear navigation with mega-menu, dropdowns on About/Services/Near You, breadcrumbs on pages, visible "Call Now" button in the header, social links in the footer, custom 404 page (with a creative deck-themed message).

Issues: the "News" link in the top bar goes to /blog, but the main menu also has "Blog" — inconsistency (News vs Blog). The About, Why Choose Us, and Our Process pages have redundant content between them — many sections repeat ("Why Homeowners Contact Loudoun Decks", the 4-step process). These could be consolidated. The mega-menu has 40+ location links — the high volume can dilute link equity.

---

## 10. PERFORMANCE — Excellent

Load time: ~235ms, DOM Content Loaded: 78ms, TTFB: 14ms. Next.js on Vercel delivers excellent performance. 17 out of 18 images are lazy-loaded. This is not a major concern.

Issue: Most images are JPEG, not WebP/AVIF. Converting would reduce file sizes by ~25-35%.

---

## 11. ACCESSIBILITY — Decent

Skip link present, lang="en" set, 19 aria-labels, all images have alt attributes. Good.
